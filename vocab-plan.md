# Vocabulary Curriculum — Spoken Cantonese (speak first → read later)

Goal: **hold simple everyday conversations in spoken Cantonese**, then begin
reading. Vocabulary-led, theme by theme. Every word learned **with its tones**
(Jyutping 1–6), every countable noun **with its classifier**, each theme = word
set + one pattern point + speaking practice out loud. Lessons are on-demand
(do several per sitting).

> **Spoken-first rule:** teach the COLLOQUIAL spoken word (食 sik6, 睇 tai2, 係
> hai6, 唔 m4, 冇 mou5, 屋企 uk1 kei2), NOT the formal written form. Jyutping is
> what Iris learns and is quizzed on. Characters are shown for the eye but not
> tested until **Phase 2 (reading)**.

Tone key (on "si"): 1 詩 high-flat · 2 史 rising · 3 試 mid-flat · 4 時 low-falling
· 5 市 low-rising · 6 事 low-flat.

Classifier key (top ones to master): **個 go3** general/people · **本 bun2** books
· **張 zoeng1** flat things (table, paper, ticket) · **杯 bui1** cups/drinks ·
**碗 wun2** bowls (rice, soup) · **隻 zek3** animals/one-of-a-pair · **架 gaa3**
vehicles/machines · **條 tiu4** long things (street, fish, trousers) · **件 gin6**
clothes/items.

## Pattern / grammar track (woven into the units above, in order)
1. **係 / 唔係** (to be / not to be) + 我係…人 (nationality)
2. **有 / 冇** (have / not have) — note 冇 mou5, not 無
3. **Classifier + noun** (一杯茶, 一本書) and **classifier with 呢/嗰** (呢杯茶 this cup of tea)
4. **想 + verb** ("want to": 我想食飯) and **要 + noun** ("want / need")
5. **Yes-no questions:** verb + 唔 + verb (食唔食呀? 好唔好?) and final 呀/嗎
6. **Question words in place** (你食乜嘢? — the word stays where the answer goes)
7. **咗 zo2** = completed action (我食咗飯) — the key "past" marker
8. **緊 gan2** = ongoing (我食緊飯 "I'm eating")
9. **Adjective + 啲 di1** comparatives (大啲 "bigger") and 最 zeoi3 (most)
10. **Connectors** — 同埋 (and) / 但係 (but) / 因為…所以… (because…so…)

## Daily routine
- **~15–25 min/day.** 1 new unit every 1–2 days; the app's spaced review handles the rest.
- Each session: review (app) → new theme words **with tones + classifier** →
  1 pattern point → 4–5 sentences English→Cantonese → **say them out loud.**
- Export the app log regularly and tell me **"sync my practice."**

## How new words reach the app
When I teach a unit, on the next sync I append its words to `practice.html`
(VOCAB with tones + classifier + characters + example, and SENTENCES), so every
theme becomes drillable. Tones feed the **Tones** tab; classifiers feed the
**Classifiers** tab. `progress.md` tracks the **Current unit**.
